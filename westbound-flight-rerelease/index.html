Westbound Flight Rerelease

A novel about love, restraint, and the price of moral collapse.

Rajagopal has everything a young Indian man is expected to want — intelligence, discipline, cultural pride, and a coveted career in America. What he does not expect is Celia.

Celia is not drawn to Raj’s success, but to his restraint.
His silences. His rituals. His refusal to become what the world around him celebrates.

As their lives intertwine across continents — from New Jersey bars to Villupuram temples — love begins to test belief, and desire begins to test identity.

But ideals are fragile.
And when discipline fractures, love does not forgive easily.

Westbound Flight is not a story about East versus West.
It is about what happens when a man loses the very values that once made him worthy of love.

Written with cultural depth and moral courage, this novel explores:

  • Cross-cultural intimacy

  • The psychology of self-control

  • The cost of betraying one’s own inner compass

Some journeys change where you live.
Others change who you become.
