Portable Characters Immovable Stories

What if characters could leave their stories—but never themselves?

On a quiet Friday evening, after a library closes, something unusual begins.
Characters from different fiction books—romantic heroes, war veterans, journalists, villains—start talking. They compare how they are read, how they are misunderstood, and who they secretly wish they were.

By the weekend, some of them make a forbidden choice:
they step into other stories.

The plots remain unchanged.
The endings stay inevitable.
Only the characters arrive… wrong.

A charming romantic hero investigates a crime through empathy.
A journalist enters a love story and treats affection like a deadline.
A war hero walks a peaceful world, bracing for a battle that never comes.

As readers begin to sense something is off, the stories push back—tightening, accelerating, demanding their endings.

Portable Characters, Immovable Stories is a quiet, philosophical novel about identity, interpretation, and the tension between who we are and the roles we’re assigned. A literary fable for readers who love stories about stories—and what happens when they refuse to stay in place.
